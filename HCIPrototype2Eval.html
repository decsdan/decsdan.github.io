UX Evaluation

Evaluation Metrics:

Task Completion Time:
The time it takes for a user to complete a specific task
Action Error Rate:
The number of times the wrong button was clicked
Navigation Error Rate:
The number of times a navigation error was made

Evaluation: Sticky Notes

2 Stakeholders (Carleton Students) were asked to complete three tasks, and were asked to note any observations, critiques, or compliments on sticky notes, and sort them into two categories: Efficiency and Clarity. While this was being done, Evaluation Metrics were recorded for each task.

Results:

Users found Navigation between pages intuitive, as there was a consistent navigation bar at the bottom, as well as clearly labeled buttons. Users found the Material Design 3 format (from Google) professional, consistent, and minimalist. There were a few issues with finding exactly what was interactible on the buy and sell pages, and the lack of dates or a specific calendar meant finding the next event caused some confusion. These issues were improved upon in my High Fidelity Prototype
